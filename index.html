One Young World Bath Caucus 2020

About the Event

This exciting free event consists of a series of international and domestic speaker talks, workshops, and networking opportunities.

The event takes place on the University of Bath campus, as a one day conference, on the 7th of March. Leading voices from business and society join students and interested local attendees in order to share ideas and engage in discussions around the issues that we face both domestically and internationally.

The conference is composed of a series of succinct speaker talks and follow-up workshops that encourage discussions on issues aligned with the One Young World values and the UN Sustainable Development Goals.

This event is by the University of Bath in partnership with One Young World, a global organisation that focuses on harnessing the power of young leaders, who demonstrate leadership ability and a commitment to effecting positive change. This year in particular, we’re witnessing an inaugural partnership between the School of Sciences and the School of Management.

Upcoming Events

Chris Hemmings

Based on experiences from his own life, and research from his book, Chris Hemmings explores how certain aspects of masculine expectation, the male pursuit of dominance and toxic masculinity are not only damaging individual men and boys, but women and girls, too.

Chris's talk will focus on sexual violence and toxic masculinity with the opportunity for Q&A, in support of some of the University’s campaigns such as #NeverOK. It’s based on his own personal story and the mistakes he made as part of his involvement in university rugby culture, and his battle with how macho tropes damaged his sense of self and took him places he is now ashamed of.

Chris wants to promote a healthier idea of what it means to be a man. For men and boys to critically examine their own behaviour, and recognise they have the power to help their brothers overcome mental health issues, tendencies for violence and the removal of female agency. He believes it's only by removing the patriarchal pressures exerted on men and boys that we can allow each other the space to be an individual - and encourage more of a positive environment where it makes us feel empowered to speak out against negative traits and injustices.

Chris is a freelance journalist who has worked for the BBC, Sky, ITV, the Independent, the Telegraph and appeared as a contributor on the BBC, CNN and ITN.

This event is organised by the Equality, Diversity & Inclusivity team who aim to build an inclusive environment while providing advice, support and guidance on equality and diversity-related issues and by One Young World Bath whose conference on 07 March 2020 will focus on finding solutions to reduce sexual violence as one of the key themes.

Keynote Speakers

Mary Akongo

Mary Helda Akongo travelled from Kampala, Uganda specifically to speak at the One Young World Bath Caucus in 2019. We are excited to announce that we have the pleasure of hosting her again for the One Young World Bath Caucus 2020.

As the founder of Roaring Doves – an online and offline peer support community for victims and survivors of gender-based violence (GBV) who come together to create awareness of GBV – Mary Helda managed to give people the chance to share their stories and support each other.

She is also the operations and programs manager for Zimba Women; a Ugandan organisation that finds innovative technological solutions to create sustainable futures for African women.

Mary Helda is also currently the One Young World Coordinating Ambassador for East Africa, in charge of managing and reporting on the Ambassador Community of the fourteen East African countries. In 2017, Mary Helda was awarded the 25 under 25 award by the Internet Society for her exceptional work with Zimba Women, which is using the Internet to create a positive impact on the lives of women in Sub-Saharan Africa. She recently won the World Bank #Blog4Dev writing competition for penning a brilliant story on her experience as a victim of GBV and her work with Roaring Doves. Mary Helda believes that technology has the power to positively influence the social, political and economic development of women in Africa.

Caroline Casey

Also a returning speaker for the One Young World Bath Caucus, Caroline Casey is an award-winning social entrepreneur and founder of #valuable - a catalyst for an inclusion revolution that exists to position disability equally on the global business leadership agenda.

Committed to building a global movement on inclusive business for the 1.3 billion people in the world with a disability, over the past two decades she has set up several organisations and initiatives centred on disability business inclusion.

One of her initiatives, The Valuable 500, is an ambitious year-long campaign to get 500 businesses to commit to put disability inclusion on their leadership agendas. Launched last year as part of the World Economic Forum Annual Summit, Casey succeeded in bringing disability inclusion onto the main stage at DAVOS for the first time ever with the support of global business leaders.

The Valuable 500 is supported by a host of global leaders including Sir Richard Branson and Paul Polman, as well as global brands including Virgin Media and Omnicom. Casey is also a TED speaker, Ashoka Fellow, Eisenhower Fellow, a past advisor for the Clinton Global Initiative, a One Young World Counsellor and is a Young Global Leader of the World Economic Forum.
